Continent

    Asia

  1. Asia is the largest continent, covering about 30% of Earth's land area and home to more than 4.7 billion people
  2. It includes 49 countries and stretches from the Arctic in the north to the Indian Ocean in the south, bordered by the Pacific and Arctic Oceans
  3. Asia has extreme landforms, such as Mount Everest (world's highest peak) and the Dead Sea (lowest land point)
  4. The continent is known for its diverse climates and landscapes, from deserts like the Gobi to tropical forests and tundra
  5. Asia is rich in cultures and ethnic groups, with thousands of languages and major world religions originating herer

    Africa

  1. Africa is the second largest continent in the world, covering about 20% of Earth's land area
  2. It is made up of 54 countries and is home to over 1.3 billion people, with a very young population
  3. The Sahara Desert (largest hot desert) and the Nile River (longest river) are found in Africa
  4. Africa is rich in natural resources and wildlife, and is known as the "cradle of humankind" because early humans originated there
  5. Over 2,000 languages are spoken across Africa, showing its great cultural and ethnic diversity

    North America

  1. Over 2,000 languages are spoken across Africa, showing its great cultural and ethnic diversity
  2. It includes 23 countries and territories, with the largest by area being Canada and the largest by population the United States
  3. The continent stretches from the Arctic Circle to near the Equator, with landscapes including mountains (like the Rockies), plains, forests, deserts, and tundra
  4. Mount Denali in Alaska is North America's highest point, and the Missouri River is its longest river
  5. North America is bordered by the Arctic Ocean (north), Atlantic Ocean (east), Pacific Ocean (west), and is connected to South America by the Isthmus of Panama

    South America

  1. South America is the fourth largest continent, spanning about 17.8 million square kilometers (6.9 million square miles)
  2. It is home to approximately 438 million people as of 2025, with Brazil being the most populous country
  3. The continent consists of 12 independent countries and several territories, featuring great cultural and ecological diversity
  4. Key geographic features include the Amazon Rainforest (world’s largest rainforest) and the Andes Mountains (longest continental mountain range
  5. South America stretches from the Caribbean Sea in the north to Cape Horn at its southern tip

    Antarctica

  1. Antarctica is the fifth largest continent, covering about 14 million square kilometers (5.5 million square miles)
  2. It has no permanent or indigenous population; only scientists and support staff live there temporarily at research stations
  3. The population varies seasonally: about 4,000–5,000 people in summer and around 1,000 in winter
  4. Antarctica’s harsh climate means it is covered mostly by ice, with no cities or regular settlements, just research bases
  5. The continent is home to unique wildlife like penguins and seals and is protected by an international treaty that bans military activity and encourages scientific research

    Europe

  1. Europe is the second smallest continent by area but the third most populous, with around 744 million people in 2025—about 9% of the world's population
  2. It is located mostly in the Northern Hemisphere and bordered by Asia, the Atlantic Ocean, the Arctic Ocean, and the Mediterranean Sea
  3. Europe has a high population density and is known for its many countries, with Russia being the largest and Germany the most populous in Central Europe
  4. The continent experiences declining population growth and an aging population, trends common in many developed regions
  5. Europe is celebrated for its rich cultural, historical, and linguistic diversity, with landmarks, traditions, and cities central to world heritage

    Australia

  1. Australia is the only country that is also a continent, covering about 7.7 million square kilometers, making it the sixth-largest country in the world
  2. Its population is nearly 28 million as of July 2025, mostly living in major cities along the eastern and southern coasts
  3. Australia is known for being highly urbanized and one of the most sparsely populated countries due to its large desert interior
  4. The country features diverse landscapes, including deserts, tropical rainforests, and a long coastline
  5. It has a unique history, with Aboriginal Australians as its original inhabitants and a culture shaped by immigration and British colonization
