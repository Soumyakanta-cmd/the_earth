Ocean

    Atlantic Ocean

  1. The Atlantic Ocean is the second largest ocean on Earth, covering about 20% of the planet's surface
  2. It lies between the continents of the Americas to the west and Europe and Africa to the east
  3. The Atlantic Ocean is roughly S-shaped and is divided into the North and South Atlantic by the Equator
  4. Its deepest point is the Puerto Rico Trench, reaching about 8,380 meters (27,493 feet)
  5. The Atlantic plays a vital role in global trade and climate, featuring major sea routes and diverse marine life

    Pacific Ocean

  1. The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest ocean on Earth, covering about one-third of its surface
  2. It stretches from the Arctic in the north to the Southern Ocean (or Antarctica) in the south and lies between Asia/Australia and the Americas
  3. Its total area is approximately 165 million square kilometers (63 million square miles)—more than all Earth's landmasses combined
  4. The deepest point on Earth, the Mariana Trench, reaching about 11,000 meters (36,000 feet), is located in the Pacific
  5. The Pacific was named by Ferdinand Magellan for its “peaceful” waters, though it is home to vast marine life and active geologic features like the “Ring of Fire”

    Indian Ocean

  1. The Indian Ocean is the third largest ocean in the world, covering about 20% of the Earth's ocean area
  2. It is bordered by Asia, Africa, Australia, and the Southern Ocean (Antarctica)
  3. The Indian Ocean is triangular in shape and is named after India on its northern border
  4. It is the warmest ocean and experiences important weather patterns like monsoons and cyclones
  5. The Indian Ocean is a major route for world trade and is home to diverse marine life such as whales and turtles

    Arctic Ocean

  1. The Arctic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest of the world's oceans, covering about 14 million square kilometers (5.4 million square miles)
  2. It is centered on the North Pole and surrounded by Eurasia, North America, Greenland, and several islands
  3. Much of the Arctic Ocean is covered by ice year-round, with the ice thickness and extent changing by season
  4. Its average depth is around 1,000 meters (3,280 feet), and the deepest point is about 5,500 meters (18,050 feet)
  5. The Arctic Ocean faces significant impacts from climate change, including shrinking sea ice and rising global importance

    Southern Ocean

  1. The Southern Ocean surrounds Antarctica and is the fourth largest ocean, larger than the Arctic but smaller than the Indian Ocean
  2. It is known for the powerful Antarctic Circumpolar Current, the strongest and longest ocean current, which rapidly circulates cold water eastward around the
  3. Sea ice expands dramatically in winter, nearly doubling Antarctica’s size, and recedes in summer, showing one of the greatest seasonal changes on Earth
  4. The Southern Ocean supports rich ecosystems, with nutrient-rich waters sustaining vast populations of phytoplankton, krill, whales, seals, and penguins
  5. It experiences some of the strongest winds and largest waves on Earth and plays a major role in global climate by regulating heat and carbon dioxide
